I tend to fix the borrowers' rate and borrowers' APR variables (also looking at the Estimated Effective Yield) and see how they are affected by numeric and categorial variables in this dataset. How does the interest rates and loan yield fare?¶

I am going to focus my analysis on 3 main variables, the borrowerrate, borrowerapr and estimatedeffective yield. The Borrower APR is typically the interest rate plus any extra fees may be charged to take out a loan. It is important to address this variable along with the normal Borrower rate.

The Estimated Effective Yield is simply the return on investment in a loan, typically for investors who are willing to fund thi loan to get returns on it depending on the interest rates and the borrowers' payoffs. There is a nice link giving some definitions about it.

Estimated Effective Yield measure, does the data show encouragement to lenders?

The Borrower Rate and the Borrower APR have a high correlation, this is expected as both are related as stated before. The Estimated Effective Yield is also highly correlated with both interest rates (~0.89), this is also expected as the borrow rates would invite returns to investors as it can be seen from the previous plot, of course the risk is still there. The Debt to Income Ratio shows a weak correlation with the Borrower Rate and Borrower APR as the coefficient is close to 0. This might mean that the interest rates might be affected more by other variables.

On the contrary, the remaining 4 variables have a negative correlation on the Borrwer Rate and the Borrwer APR. The Prosper Score has the highest negative correlation with the 3 rates, so with a Prosper getting a higher score they are most likely recieve a lesser rate and the opposite so. These Borrowers with high scores are most likely able to settle their debts, and they need an attractive rate to apply for a loan, while that's appealing the risk factor being lowered investors will be more appealed to invest on these borrowers.

Though slightly negative correlation as the 3 rates are lowered, a safer harbor to investors. Meaning they can expect much lower yield but assured return, and this is more apparent when you look at the previous plots, the risk factor is higher as the credit grade is lower due to the outliers present. But as the Prosper scores and credit grades get higher, the yield is dramatically decreased but with much less outliers, the slight increase in the investors will occur given the negative correlation of the investors with the estimatedeffectiveyield. This can be further justified by the positive correlation between the prosperscore and the investors.

The Loan Original Amount has a mildly negative correlation. That is, if you go to get a high value loan, your interest rates will be lowered and thus the estimated yield. My thoughts here is that becuase borrowers who are likely having high value loans tend to spread the loans on longer terms, with more chances and better competency of paying these loans, look at the loanoriginalamount interacting with the statedmonthlyincome, though slight positive correlation, these borrowers do achieve higher scores and attract more investors.

Finally, the Loan percent funded does not have a significant effect as the coefficient is close to 0. You can predict that this weak chane is because the range of funding percentage is between 70% and 100%.

  • Prosper Score (post July 2009)

Post the economic crisis, it was predictable to start having a higher mean for the rates with higher variability as an aftermath, with more can be associated to inflations, economic fixes and bad debts, the rates continued an increase to a sky high ~30%. Then steadly falling down to reach a post economic crisis low of ~20% average and going less.

The effects seen can be attributed to the Debt Consolidation listings as borrowers at that time did not have a settling income against the crazy inflation rates and inability to pay off current debts. That has streamed the increase of the rates until economic fixes took an effect althrough till 2014 and beyond. The Federal reserve then decided on steady growths in rates in a bid to improve more on economic activities according to this article in the NY times.

Key Takeouts from the analysis¶

There are some interesting conclusions and insights that were seen here. I wanted to see the borrowing interest rates with all variables, now I can conclude the following.

Many of the borrowers are burdened by borrowing interest rates over the course of time. Apart from loan facilities, most of these individuals are relatively young given the years of experience, and with income ranges at the middle.

These individuals seem to usually carry on lives through debts, since loan listings show an increase in loan amounts being taken by the year with the dominant factor is settling other debts (Debt Consolidation). This closed loop of lending is a worrying signal as neither do (nearly) half of these people have a collateral other than their salary.
