Motivation¶

The pulled variables in my polished dataset are the main aspects I want to look at. Fristly, I want to understand the profile of the listed borrowers, and their main motive behind. Also, how does the prospers fare in terms of gains for investors who are willing to take part in investing on loans, what other variables might influence them?

I tend to fix the borrowers' rate and borrowers' APR variables (also looking at the Estimated Effective Yield) and see how they are affected by numeric and categorial variables in this dataset. The Borrower Rate and the Borrower APR have a high correlation, this is expected as both are related as stated before. The Estimated Effective Yield is also highly correlated with both interest rates (~0.89), this is also expected as the borrow rates would invite returns to investors as it can be seen from the previous plot, of course the risk is still there. The Debt to Income Ratio shows a weak correlation with the Borrower Rate and Borrower APR as the coefficient is close to 0. This might mean that the interest rates might be affected more by other variables.

On the contrary, the remaining 4 variables have a negative correlation on the Borrwer Rate and the Borrwer APR. The Prosper Score has the highest negative correlation with the 3 rates, so with a Prosper getting a higher score they are most likely recieve a lesser rate and the opposite so. These Borrowers with high scores are most likely able to settle their debts, and they need an attractive rate to apply for a loan, while that's appealing the risk factor being lowered investors will be more appealed to invest on these borrowers.

Though slightly negative correlation as the 3 rates are lowered, a safer harbor to investors. Meaning they can expect much lower yield but assured return, and this is more apparent when you look at the previous plots, the risk factor is higher as the credit grade is lower due to the outliers present. But as the Prosper scores and credit grades get higher, the yield is dramatically decreased but with much less outliers, the slight increase in the investors will occur given the negative correlation of the investors with the estimatedeffectiveyield. This can be further justified by the positive correlation between the prosperscore and the investors.

The Loan Original Amount has a mildly negative correlation. That is, if you go to get a high value loan, your interest rates will be lowered and thus the estimated yield. My thoughts here is that becuase borrowers who are likely having high value loans tend to spread the loans on longer terms, with more chances and better competency of paying these loans, look at the loanoriginalamount interacting with the statedmonthlyincome, though slight positive correlation, these borrowers do achieve higher scores and attract more investors.

Finally, the Loan percent funded does not have a significant effect as the coefficient is close to 0. You can predict that this weak chane is because the range of funding percentage is between 70% and 100%.
